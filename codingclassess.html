Code First:Girls

Code First: Girls is a not for profit multi-award winning women-only social enterprise which focuses on teaching young women around UK how to code. Their main objective is to increase the number of women in tech and to address gender imbalance in the tech industry.

Revolut

FinTech company Revolut announced that it will host free coding classes for women at its head office in Canary Wharf, London. Classes are limited to 15-20 students and are recruiting on monthly bases to ensure that those partaking can get as much one-on-one support as possible.

Ladies of Code

If you are you searching for a bunch of ladies to discuss code with you came to right place! This group of ladies meets up monthly in London to hear talks from leaders in the tech field whilst improving their code-fu in the process.

Women Who Code

Women Who Code is not for a profit organisation, whose mission is to inspire women to excel in technology careers. Once a member you gain access to programs and services that are designed to help women to step up their tech career, including a network of over 167,000 female tech professionals.

Codebar

Codebar is a non-profit initiative supporting underrepresented people in learning programming in a safe and collaborative environment to expand their career opportunities. Codebar are present in Brighton, Cambridge, Manchester, Bournemouth, Edinburgh, Glasgow, Oxford Kent, Norwich, Peterborough and Bristol.

Rails Girls

Rails Girls was born in Finland, but is nowadays a global, non-profit volunteer community providing women with the tools and community to understand technology and to build their ideas. The non-profit offers opportunities to learn coding in Ruby, Python, and other languages by offering free workshops around the world.

Django Girls

Django Girls is a non-profit organisation and a community that empowers and helps women to organise free, one-day programming workshops by providing tools, resources and support. During each of the events, 30-60 women build their first web application using HTML, CSS, Python and Django.

23 Code Street

If you would like to learn how to code, but are also interested in giving back then 23 Code Street is for you! They are a coding school for all women, where for every paying student they teach digital skills to a woman inthe slums of India. Ypour learning process will consist of a series of events and webinars of your choice.
